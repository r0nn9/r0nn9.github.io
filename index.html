About Me

I’m a college freshman at Holy Cross College and I’m loving the chance to explore new experiences and meet new people. I grew up in Indianapolis, which gave me a lot of opportunities to play sports and stay active from a young age. I really enjoy playing soccer, baseball, and pickleball, and being on the field or court is one of my favorite ways to spend time. When I’m not playing sports, I love relaxing by playing video games, watching my favorite shows, or just hanging out with friends. Soccer has always been a big part of my life, teaching me teamwork, focus, and perseverance, and I try to bring that same energy to everything I do. I’m always up for trying new things, whether it’s exploring new hobbies, traveling, or challenging myself in school.

Personal Experience

I started playing soccer when I was about 5 years old, but at first I didn’t even want to play. I actually wanted to play basketball instead. Over time, though, I stuck with soccer and began to understand the game more. At the beginning, I didn’t really know what I was doing, but as I kept playing, I became smarter and more confident on the field.

The next year, I started playing for club teams. I played defense and I liked it, but I always wanted to play midfield. I would constantly ask my coach to move me there, but he never did. Even though it was frustrating, it pushed me to keep working harder. I wanted to be the best, especially because my older brothers were really good and they were my inspiration when I was younger.

After that, I joined a better team. I thought I was improving, but I quickly realized that everyone else was getting better faster than I was. That made me nervous because I felt like I might not get much playing time. Eventually, I ended up trying a new position (goalkeeper) and everything changed.

Playing goalkeeper felt natural to me, and I became really good at it. I started making big plays, helping my team win games, and gaining confidence. One of my favorite moments was winning sectionals as a freshman with my brother. After years of trying different positions, I finally found where I belonged on the field, and that made the experience even more meaningful.

Soccer History

Soccer is one of the oldest and most popular sports in the world, and it has a history that’s really cool to look at. People have been playing ball games with their feet for hundreds of years, way back in ancient civilizations. The version we know today really started in England in the 1800s when the first official rules were written in 1863. These rules made the game more organized and helped teams compete fairly. Fun fact: the U.S. is the only country that calls it “soccer”, while pretty much everywhere else in the world calls it “football”. It’s amazing to think that a simple idea of kicking a ball turned into something so huge. One of the most exciting parts of soccer’s history is all the big tournaments and legendary moments. The FIFA World Cup started in 1930 and has brought some of the most unforgettable games ever. Clubs and leagues all over the world, like in Europe and South America, helped make soccer even more exciting and competitive. Legendary players like Pelé, Maradona, and Messi have made the sport unforgettable for fans of all ages. Soccer history is full of stories of incredible teamwork, amazing goals, and intense rivalries. Learning about all this makes me even more passionate about the game I love to play today.
